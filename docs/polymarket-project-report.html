Key Finding

Strategies Tested

Each strategy was designed with a clear thesis, validated through empirical data, and either deployed live or killed based on results — never on speculation.

Strategy 1 — Pre-Game Passive Market Making
Negative EV

Thesis: Quote both sides of pre-game sports markets, capture the bid-ask spread, earn maker rebates (25% of taker fee), and exit flat before game start.

Result: 36 maker fills across 5 live sessions (Apr 3-7). Round-trip completion rate ~37%. Direction accuracy of fills showed consistent adverse selection — we only get filled when the market is about to move against us. SOFT_CLOSE works mechanically but can't force counterparty fills.

Live Session Data (Apr 3-7): Total fills: 36 | Round-trips: 7 Profitable round-trips: 1 | Breakeven: 2 | Stop-loss: 4 Unhedged exits: ~18 (SOFT_CLOSE deadlock) Starting balance: $28.03 → Peak: $30.08 → Final: $28.68 Root cause: Adverse selection. In 1-3c spread markets, institutional MMs have queue priority. We get filled only when the market has already moved against our price.
Strategy A — Directional Taker (Pinnacle Calibration)
No Edge

Thesis: Polymarket US sports markets are mispriced vs. sharp sportsbook lines (Pinnacle). Build a probability model, find delta > taker fees, and execute as aggressive taker.

Result: Integrated Pinnacle, FanDuel, DraftKings odds via The-Odds-API. Built de-vigging module (multiplicative method) and team matching for 100+ teams. Cross-platform delta averaged 0.8%, well below the ~2% taker fee threshold.

Cross-Platform Calibration (Apr 2): NBA: avg |delta| = 0.8% — BELOW 2% fee threshold NHL: avg |delta| = 0.6% — BELOW 2% fee threshold MLB: avg |delta| = 0.2% — BELOW 2% fee threshold MMA: avg |delta| = 1.0% — BELOW 2% fee threshold Max delta observed: 2.8% (single MMA fight) Markets with delta > 3%: 0 Conclusion: Polymarket US tracks Pinnacle within ±0.8%. No directional taker edge survives fees.
Strategy 3 — Cross-Market Correlation
50% Accuracy

Thesis: When a moneyline market moves, correlated spread/totals markets on the same game lag by seconds to minutes. Detect the leader move and taker-execute the follower before it reprices.

Result: 24h of data (354K+ snapshots, 59 events, 177 markets). Timing lag is real — 30-50% of moneyline moves take 60s+ to propagate. But direction accuracy is ~50% (coin flip). The lag is uncorrelated noise, not information propagation.

Direction Accuracy Analysis (Apr 6-7, 354K snapshots): MLB moneyline→spread: 50.0% accuracy (coin flip) MLB moneyline→totals: 51.3% accuracy NBA moneyline→spread: 57.1% accuracy (best, but...) Simulated PnL (all pairs, all sports): NEGATIVE MLB best case: -2.4c avg per trade NHL worst case: -18.4c avg per trade Conclusion: Lag exists but direction is unpredictable. Totals markets ≠ directional derivative of moneyline.
Kalshi Market Making
Hibernated

Thesis: Same MM strategy on Kalshi's CFTC-regulated event contracts.

Result: Scanner found 0 markets passing filters. Net spread after maker fees: 0-2c vs Polymarket's 1.2-1.8c with maker rebates. Kalshi charges makers, Polymarket pays makers. Structurally inferior.

Key Learnings

Lessons applicable to any retail quantitative trading project, regardless of asset class.

Market Structure

Efficient markets are efficient. Polymarket US sports pricing tracks Pinnacle within ±0.8%. Spreads are 1-3c. The market is already being serviced by institutional MMs with superior capital, infrastructure, and queue priority. There is no free lunch in well-trafficked sports markets.

Adverse selection is the #1 killer of retail MMs. When you get filled, it's because an informed participant knows your price is wrong. The hedging fill never comes because the market has already moved. This is a structural disadvantage that no algorithm can overcome without capital/speed advantages.

Cross-market lag ≠ cross-market predictability. We proved that totals markets lag moneyline moves by 60-300s. But the direction of the lagging move is a coin flip. Lag without directional signal is noise, not alpha.

Engineering

Fill detection must use exchange-confirmed data. Inferring fills from order disappearance is fundamentally unreliable. The activities/trades API is the only source of truth.

Test in production, not just on paper. Paper trading showed +$8.35 in 4 sessions. Live trading found 5 critical bugs in the first 3 sessions. Paper success ≠ live success.

Data > theory, always. Every strategy pivot was driven by empirical results, not speculation. Strategy A was killed by calibration data. Cross-market correlation was killed by direction accuracy data. This discipline saved time and capital.

Process

Adversarial code review: 2 rounds max. Gemini (secondary reviewer) provided useful catches in rounds 1-2 but diminishing returns after. Cross-reference secondary opinions against empirical data rather than accepting them directly.

Conclusion & Recommendations

The project successfully validated that Polymarket US sports markets are too efficient for retail-scale automated trading. The infrastructure built is production-grade and transferable.

What Worked

The engineering methodology — paper validate → live deploy → diagnose from logs → fix with tests → iterate — was highly effective. We went from zero to live trading in 5 days, and from "zero fills" to "fills were always happening but undetected" in one debugging session. The empirical rigor in killing strategies saved weeks of wasted effort.

What Didn't Work

The fundamental assumption that a retail trader with $25 can profitably market-make on institutional-grade sports prediction markets. The market microstructure (1-3c spreads, queue priority for large orders, Pinnacle-calibrated pricing) makes this structurally impossible without either significant capital ($10K+) for queue priority or ultra-low-latency infrastructure for in-play trading.

This project spent $0 learning that an edge doesn't exist — arguably the most valuable outcome. The alternative (deploying $10K without this validation) would have been far more expensive. The infrastructure, methodology, and empirical discipline built here are the real deliverables.
